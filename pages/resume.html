Project

WTFShouldIBuild

Visit

WTFShouldIbuild.com was born when I was sitting at my desk one day wondering what I should build next to help improve my skills. So I started to search the web for some project ideas and making my list.

The problem

The problem was that I spent so much time jumping around from different sites to find some projects to work on. I spent more time looking for that perfect project, instead of just picking one to start building. I thought to myslef, this sure would be easier if their was one website that would give me ideas.

The solution

I decided to take my list that I had gathered and create a website around it. Instead of a simple list for a user to scroll through, I decided to go with a single card with one item on it. With a click of a button an item is selected at random and shown to the user. This way the user can focus on one item and decide if this is a project they want to take on. It also does not tie any particular language or design on how it should be built. It will allow the developer to make those choices and help with their critical thinking skills. I also added a form for other users to share their ideas and get them added to the list. This way they can help grow the list and will hopefully be young developers website of choice to find ideas to help improve their skills.

Texas Pool & Spa

Visit

Texas Pool & Spa is a family owned company who service pools and spas in texas.

The Problem

Texas pool & Spa had a website that was a little outdated and non-responsive. This affected their ranking on Google search because the site was not mobile friendly and with Google's new ranking algorithm their site was being penelized. This was affecting their ability to bring in new clients in the Texas area.

The Solution

I started off with a complete re-design of the website. Making sure the new design was going to be responsive and fit any device customers used to view the website on. This would also fix the issue with not being recognized by Google's ranking algorithm for mobile. The next step was creating a new design that would be inviting and professional looking for customers, while also meeting the clients needs for a website.
